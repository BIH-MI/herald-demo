HERALD Project - Web Demo 

HERALD (Human-centric Extraction for Research and Analysis of Longitudinal Data) is a query language designed to facilitate the extraction and aggregation of longitudinal health data into cross-sectional tables suitable for statistical analysis and machine learning. It provides self-service analytical access for medical researchers. The prototype consists of a HERALD execution engine, combined with a graphical query editor, and paired with analytics functionalities to demonstrate the capabilities of the HERALD query language. Below you'll find three different examples deployed within a web demo.

Example 1: Coronary Heart Disease vs. Diabetes

Explore a comparative analysis between two cohorts of patients with a primary diagnosis of either coronary heart disease or type 2 diabetes. The dataset allows the study of demographic variables (age, sex) and common risk factors (BMI, LDL and HDL cholesterol levels) as well as comorbidity profiles.

Open
Example 2: Alzheimer's Disease

Investigate Alzheimer's disease by comparing early-onset patients with those diagnosed at a later stage. The dataset provided includes basic demographic information (age, sex), Mini-Mental State Examination (MMSE) scores to assess cognitive function, pneumonia incidence, care plans and medications.

Open
Example 3: Lung Cancer

Contrast patient cohorts diagnosed with non-small cell carcinoma of the lung of TNM stages 1 to 4, who have received combined chemotherapy and radiation therapy. Additional information on basic demographics (age, sex), BMI, related medication and selected lab values (Hemoglobin, Glucose and eGFR) is provided.
